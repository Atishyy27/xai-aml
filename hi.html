The Investigator's Challenge: From Data Overload to Actionable Intelligence

Our solution is built to address the core operational pain points faced by Investigating Officers (IOs) in the fight against financial crime.

The Current Workflow: A Needle in a Haystack

  • **Data Dumps:** IOs receive disconnected lists of thousands of transactions.
  • **Alert Fatigue:** Legacy systems generate over 95% false positives, burying real threats in noise.
  • **Hidden Conspiracies:** Sophisticated layering and mule networks are nearly impossible to trace manually.
  • **Lack of Context:** Alerts lack the "why," forcing IOs to start every investigation from scratch.

The SENTINEL Workflow: Intelligence on Demand

  • **Automated Network Mapping:** Data is instantly structured as a graph to reveal hidden relationships.
  • **Prioritized Intelligence:** The AI delivers a ranked list of the most suspicious *networks*, not just transactions.
  • **Interactive Visualization:** IOs can visually "follow the money" through an intuitive graph interface.
  • **Evidence-Based Reports:** Every alert is backed by a plain-English XAI report, explaining the "why" for faster case-building.

Our Solution: A Hybrid, Explainable AI Core

SENTINEL's innovation lies in its multi-stage, research-backed architecture that synthesizes the most effective AML techniques.

Stage 1: Unsupervised Anomaly Detection

An **Autoencoder** neural network learns the "normal" transactional DNA of every account. It acts as a first-pass filter, flagging individual accounts that exhibit statistically unusual behavior without needing prior examples of fraud.

Inspired by unsupervised methods for finding novel patterns.

Stage 2: Supervised Network Detection

A **Graph Convolutional Network (GCN)** analyzes the connections between accounts. Trained on known laundering typologies, it detects entire criminal conspiracies, identifying network structures like layering chains and mule rings that are invisible to single-account analysis.

Based on graph-based detection in FlowScope (Li et al., 2020) & Weber et al. (2019).

Stage 3: Explainable AI (XAI) Engine

The **SHAP framework** translates the complex decisions of our AI models into a human-readable report. It pinpoints the exact features that contributed to a network's risk score, providing transparent and defensible intelligence.

Addresses the critical need for interpretability cited in Kute et al. (2021).

System Architecture

A modular, containerized architecture designed for scalability, parallel development, and rapid deployment.

AI Methodology Deep Dive

Our two-stage pipeline is designed to maximize both detection accuracy and interpretability.

1. Feature Engineering

We extract a rich set of features for each account from the graph, including transactional stats (volume, velocity, net flow) and topological properties (in/out-degree), creating a detailed behavioral fingerprint.

Informed by Dumitrescu et al. (2022).

2. Autoencoder Anomaly Score

The Autoencoder learns a model of "normalcy." Accounts that deviate from this norm produce a high reconstruction error, flagging them as initial candidates for deeper analysis. This is our first-level, unsupervised sentinel.

Validated for financial anomaly detection.

3. GCN Network Classification

The GCN analyzes the flagged account and its neighbors, learning the structural patterns of criminal networks. It provides the final risk score, confirming if an individual anomaly is part of a larger, coordinated conspiracy.

Inspired by Weber et al. (2019).

Feasibility & Project Roadmap

Feasibility

  • **Modular Architecture:** Enables parallel development, ideal for a time-constrained hackathon.
  • **Proven Technologies:** Relies on established, well-documented open-source tools.
  • **Data Independence:** Synthetic data generator unblocks all development without needing sensitive data.

Risks & Mitigations

Risk: GCN training is time-intensive.
Mitigation: Train on a smaller graph for MVP; run full training in the background.

Risk: Integrating three complex components.
Mitigation: A strict, API-first contract ensures seamless integration.

Hackathon Roadmap

  • **Hours 37-40:** Final Polish, Demo Prep & Submission.
